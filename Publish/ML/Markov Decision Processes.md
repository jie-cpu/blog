# Summary 

Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) are a way to model decision-making where outcomes are partly random and partly controlled by a decision-maker.
1. **States**: Different situations the system can be in.
2. **Actions**: Choices the decision-maker can make.
3. **Transition Probabilities**: Chances of moving from one state to another after an action.
4. **Rewards**: Points or feedback received after moving to a new state.
5. **[[Policy]]**: A plan that tells what action to take in each state.
