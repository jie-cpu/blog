# Summary 

- Dynamic programming is a method for solving complex problems by breaking them into smaller parts.
1. **Overlapping Subproblems**: It saves answers to smaller problems to avoid solving them multiple times.
2. **Optimal Substructure**: The best solution to a problem can be made from the best solutions to its smaller parts.
3. **Approaches**:
    - **Top-Down (Memoization)**: Solves problems step by step and remembers answers.
    - **Bottom-Up (Tabulation)**: Starts with small problems and builds up to larger ones.
