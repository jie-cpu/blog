# Summary 

**Overfitting** in machine learning occurs when a model is **too complex** and learns the training data, including its noise and irrelevant details, **too well**.

Its core symptom is that the model performs **exceptionally well on the training data but poorly on new, unseen data**.

- **Memorization vs. Generalization:** Instead of learning general patterns, the model essentially "memorizes" the training examples. It fails to generalize to data it hasn't seen before.
- **High Variance:** Overfitting often indicates high "variance," meaning the model is too sensitive to the specific training data and doesn't capture the true underlying relationship.
- **Symptoms:** Low training error but high validation/test error. The model is too tailored to the specific quirks of the training set.
In essence, overfitting is like a student who memorizes every answer from the practice test perfectly, but then performs poorly on the actual exam because the questions are slightly different and they haven't truly understood the underlying concepts.
