# Summary 

In the context of machine learning model performance, **Bias** refers to the **error introduced by approximating a real-world problem with a simplified model**.

Its core characteristic is that the model makes **strong, often incorrect, assumptions** about the form of the relationship between input features and the output.

- **Systematic Error:** Bias represents the difference between the average prediction of our model and the correct value that we are trying to predict. It's a systematic error that the model consistently makes.
- **Too Simple:** A model with high bias is typically **too simple** or constrained, failing to capture the underlying complexity of the data. This often leads to **underfitting**.
- **Symptoms:** High training error and high testing error. The model consistently misses the true patterns.
In essence, bias is how far off a model's average prediction is from the actual truth. A high-bias model is like trying to fit a straight line to data that clearly follows a complex curve – it's just too simple to get it right.
