# Summary 

**Reinforcement Learning (RL)** is a type of machine learning where an **[[agent]] learns to make optimal decisions by interacting with an environment**.

Its core purpose is to **find a [[Policy]] that maximizes a cumulative reward** over time, without being explicitly told what to do.

- **Agent:** The learner or decision-maker.
- **[[Environment]]:** The world with which the agent interacts.
- **Actions:** What the agent can do in the environment.
- **States:** The current situation or observation of the environment.
- **Rewards:** Feedback from the environment, indicating how good or bad an action was.
In essence, reinforcement learning is like training a dog: the dog (agent) tries different actions in its surroundings (environment), receives a treat (positive reward) for desired behaviors or a scolding (negative reward) for undesirable ones, and learns over time which actions lead to the most treats. The goal is to learn the best sequence of actions to achieve a long-term objective.
