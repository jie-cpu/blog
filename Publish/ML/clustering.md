# Summary 

**Clustering** is an **unsupervised learning** task in machine learning.

Its core purpose is to **group similar data points together into clusters**, without any prior knowledge of what those groups should be.

- **Unlabeled Data:** The model works with data that has no predefined categories or labels.
- **Similarity-Based Grouping:** It identifies inherent structures or patterns by finding data points that are more similar to each other than to those in other groups. The definition of "similarity" is determined by the algorithm.
- **Discovery of Groups:** The goal is to discover natural groupings or segments within the data.
In essence, clustering is like sorting a pile of mixed toys (unlabeled data) into different bins (clusters) based on their shared characteristics (e.g., all blocks together, all cars together), without anyone telling you beforehand what categories exist or what each toy is.
