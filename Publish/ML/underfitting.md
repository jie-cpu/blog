# Summary 

**Underfitting** in machine learning occurs when a model is **too simple** to capture the underlying patterns and relationships in the training data.

Its core symptom is that the model performs **poorly on both the training data and new, unseen data**.

- **Failure to Learn:** The model hasn't learned enough from the training examples to make accurate predictions. It's like trying to explain a complex phenomenon with a very basic rule.
- **High Bias:** Underfitting often indicates high "[[bias]]," meaning the model makes strong, incorrect assumptions about the data.
- **Symptoms:** High training error and high validation/test error. The model misses obvious trends.
In essence, underfitting is like a student who hasn't studied enough for an exam and therefore performs poorly on both the practice questions and the actual test. The model is too simplistic to understand the "lesson."
