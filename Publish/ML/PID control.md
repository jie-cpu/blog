# Summary 

The core of PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) control is to maintain a desired output by continuously adjusting the control input based on the error between a setpoint and a measured process variable.
- P (Proportional): Reacts to the current error. Increases responsiveness.
- I (Integral): Accounts for accumulated past errors. Reduces steady-state errors.
- D (Derivative): Responds to the rate of change of error. Helps to prevent overshooting.
In summary, a PID controller combines these three components to provide a balanced and effective control strategy for dynamic systems.
