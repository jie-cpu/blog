
## I. The Kite Runner

### 1. Word Breakdown

| Word | Part of Speech | Literal Meaning | Deeper Meaning |
| ---- | -------------- | --------------- | -------------- |
| **Kite** | Noun | a light toy flown in the wind | Symbol of **childhood, innocence, loyalty, redemption** |
| **Runner** | Noun | one who runs or chases | A person who chases fallen kites; also one who **pursues something throughout life** |

> 📌 *Kite* comes from Old English *cyta* (a bird of prey), named because its flight resembles a hovering bird.  
> 📌 *Runner* = *run* + suffix *-er* (agent noun — one who performs an action).

---

### 2. Story Background

| Element | Details |
| ------- | ------- |
| **Time** | 1970s – early 2000s |
| **Place** | Afghanistan (Kabul) → USA (California) → Afghanistan (Kabul) |
| **Historical Context** | Soviet invasion of Afghanistan (1979); Afghan civil war; rise of the **Taliban** regime (1996–2001); 9/11 attacks and US invasion |
| **Social Context** | Rigid ethnic hierarchy in Afghanistan: **Pashtuns** (dominant ethnic group, privileged class) vs **Hazaras** (ethnic minority, marginalized and discriminated against) |
| **Cultural Context** | Traditional Afghan **kite fighting** (Gudiparan Bazi) — the biggest winter event in Kabul. Participants coat their strings with powdered glass to cut opponents' lines. **Kite runners** chase the fallen kites as trophies. |

---

### 3. The Title's Metaphorical Layers

| Layer | Interpretation |
| ----- | -------------- |
| **Literal** | A boy who chases fallen kites through the streets of Kabul |
| **Story-Level** | The protagonist spends his adult life **chasing redemption** — the "kite" he pursues is his guilt and his need for forgiveness |
| **Universal** | Every person spends their life **chasing something** — a dream, a person, a memory, or atonement |
