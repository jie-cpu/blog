## II. To Kill a Mockingbird

### 1. Word Breakdown

| Word | Part of Speech | Literal Meaning | Deeper Meaning |
| ---- | -------------- | --------------- | -------------- |
| **Kill** | Verb | to cause death | A **violent, harsh** word placed next to something innocent, creating a powerful contrast |
| **Mockingbird** | Noun | a North American bird known for imitating other birds' songs | Symbol of **innocence, harmlessness, beauty** |

> 📌 *Mockingbird* = *mock* (to imitate) + *bird* — literally **"the bird that imitates others."**  
> 📌 It does not destroy crops, does not nest in barns, and **only sings for us** — therefore killing it is a **sin**.

---

### 2. Story Background

| Element | Details |
| ------- | ------- |
| **Time** | 1930s (the Great Depression era) |
| **Place** | **Maycomb** — a fictional small town in Alabama, USA (Deep South) |
| **Historical Context** | The American South operated under **racial segregation** (Jim Crow laws). Black people were denied equal rights. The **Scottsboro Boys case** (1931) — nine Black teenagers falsely accused of raping two white women — directly inspired Harper Lee's novel |
| **Social Context** | Deep-rooted **racism** among white Southerners; rigid social class hierarchy; gender roles; the tradition of lynching (extrajudicial murder by mobs); the "honor culture" of the South |
| **Cultural Context** | Puritan morality; small-town gossip culture; the ideal of the **Southern gentleman**; the tension between traditional values and changing times |

---

### 3. The Title's Central Metaphor — What Does the Mockingbird Symbolize?

| Trait of the Mockingbird | Symbolic Meaning |
| ------------------------ | ----------------- |
| Only sings and brings beauty | **Innocence, goodness, joy** |
| Does not destroy crops or gardens | **Harmless — poses no threat to anyone** |
| Killing it is described as a "sin" | **Destroying innocence is the ultimate moral crime** |

**The title's core message:** *"It is a sin to harm the innocent."* The novel explores this through two parallel cases:
- **Tom Robinson** — a Black man falsely accused of a crime he did not commit
- **Boo Radley** — a reclusive neighbor unfairly feared and gossiped about

Both are "mockingbirds" — innocent beings destroyed by prejudice and fear.

---

### 4. Key Ideas (Keywords)

| Keyword | Meaning |
| ------- | ------- |
| **Prejudice** | pre-judging someone without knowing the facts |
| **Injustice** | unfair treatment; the denial of what is right |
| **Empathy** | the ability to understand and share another's feelings |
| **Integrity** | the quality of standing by one's moral principles |
| **Racism** | discrimination and oppression based on race |
| **Courage** | moral bravery — doing the right thing even when it is unpopular |
| **Innocence** | the state of being pure, naive, and uncorrupted |
| **Hypocrisy** | saying one thing while doing the opposite (e.g., preaching Christian values while practicing racism) |
| **Education** | the power of learning to overcome ignorance |
| **Growth** | the process of maturing and seeing the world clearly |
| **Justice** | the ideal of fairness and truth prevailing |
| **Compassion** | deep awareness of and sympathy for another's suffering |

> 🏆 **Key Line:** *"You never really understand a person until you consider things from his point of view... until you climb into his skin and walk around in it."*  
> **One-sentence summary:** Prejudice destroys the innocent; courage defends them; protecting the vulnerable is humanity's highest moral duty.
