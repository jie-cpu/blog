1. HPC — Conceptual Aspects
- HPC (High Performance Computing) focuses more on conceptual aspects to deal with large-scale computing problems, rather than on specific frameworks or tools.
- The goal is to understand the paradigms that are used in frameworks.
1. Example: Spark
- Spark is an example framework that uses the paradigm of lazy evaluation.
- It’s important to know:
    - What algorithm is used,
    - How it scales in a distributed system,
    - How to start the process,
    - When to stop,
    - How to finish the process.
    3. General Idea
- Focus on understanding:
    - What paradigms are used, regardless of the framework,
    - Crucial aspects of distributed and high-performance computing,
    - Conceptual thinking more than practical tools.
