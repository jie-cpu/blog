## Critical Conclusions

### 1. Correlation ≠ Causation

Just because two variables move together does **not** mean one causes the other.


Ice cream sales ↑ and Drowning incidents ↑
        ↓
   Both caused by HOT WEATHER (confounding factor)

### 2. Aggregation → Information Loss

Summary statistics hide details and patterns.

| What you lose | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| Outliers | Hidden by mean/average |
| Distribution shape | Lost in single numbers |
| Subgroup patterns | [[Simpson's Paradox]] |

### 3. Don't Trust Large Data Alone

> [!warning]
> Large scale + heterogeneous sources = more potential problems

| Risk | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Bias** | Systematic errors regardless of size |
| **Inconsistency** | Different formats, definitions, quality |
| **Hidden heterogeneity** | Mixing incompatible populations |

## One-Line Summary

> Big data doesn't guarantee truth — question correlation, aggregation, and data provenance.
